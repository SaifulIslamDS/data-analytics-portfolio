# Business Insights: Marketing Campaign Performance Analysis

## Executive Summary

This project analyzed 200,000 marketing campaign records to evaluate campaign ROI, conversion performance, customer segments, channel effectiveness, and cost efficiency.

The business generated an estimated $12.52B in revenue from approximately $2.50B acquisition cost, based on the dataset's ROI metric. The average ROI was 5.00, average conversion rate was 8.01%, and overall CTR was 9.98%.

## KPI Summary

| Average Conversion Rate | 8.01% |
| Overall CTR | 9.98% |
| Average CPC | $22.74 |
| Average Cost per Conversion | $284.04 |

## Key Findings

### 1. Overall campaign performance is strong but optimization is still possible

The campaign portfolio shows an average ROI of 5.00. However, averages alone are not enough. Channel, campaign type, and segment-level analysis are needed to understand where performance can be improved.

### 2. Website is the most cost-efficient channel

Website campaigns have the lowest cost per conversion, which makes them an important channel for efficient customer acquisition.

### 3. Facebook has the strongest average ROI among channels

Facebook campaigns show the highest average ROI among channels, suggesting that this channel may be useful for ROI-focused campaigns.

### 4. Influencer campaigns perform strongly

Influencer campaigns show the strongest average ROI among campaign types. This suggests that influencer-based campaigns may be worth testing further, especially for suitable customer segments.

### 5. Customer segment targeting matters

Foodies and Tech Enthusiasts perform strongly by average ROI and estimated profitability. These segments should be considered for future targeted campaigns.

### 6. Monthly performance is stable

Monthly ROI remains relatively stable across the year. September shows the highest average ROI, but the overall trend does not show extreme seasonality.

### 7. Conversion rate drives cost efficiency

Correlation analysis shows that higher conversion rate is strongly associated with lower cost per conversion. Campaigns should be optimized not only for clicks but for actual conversion performance.

## Business Recommendations

1. Increase investment in channels with stronger cost efficiency, especially Website campaigns.

2. Continue testing Facebook campaigns because they show the strongest average ROI among channels.

3. Use Influencer and Search campaigns for campaigns where ROI is the main objective.

4. Improve customer targeting for high-performing segments such as Foodies and Tech Enthusiasts.

5. Monitor campaigns with high acquisition cost and low conversion rate because they create inefficient spend.

6. Use a balanced KPI framework including ROI, conversion rate, CTR, cost per click, cost per conversion, and engagement score.

7. Review underperforming campaigns before increasing budget, especially when acquisition cost is high but conversion performance is weak.

## Final Recommendation

The marketing team should shift from campaign-volume reporting to performance-efficiency reporting. Budget decisions should be based on ROI, conversion rate, customer segment, and cost per conversion instead of clicks or impressions alone.
